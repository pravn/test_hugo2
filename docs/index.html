Poisson approximation

We take a look at the Poisson distribution in this post. Continuing on similar lines as in the previous post, we derive the MGF of this distribution. Then we make the connection between the Poisson and the Binomial. This is a cute derivation from Casella and Berger. The Poisson distribution takes on the following form: $$ \begin{aligned} p(x;\lambda) = \frac {e^{-\lambda} \lambda^x}{x!} \end{aligned} $$ We can see that it is a product of a polynomial and exponential decay term, which are sort of competing influences.
